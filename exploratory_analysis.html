Exploratory Analysis

Living situation

Plotting of participant view of living situation predictor against PHQ-2, stratified by race (EM) to show the differences in PHQ-2 (depression risk scale) distribution across categories.

Perceived Overall Health

Visualizing any differences in depression outcome by predictor, perceived overall health.

  • The respondents were asked to rate their overall health on a scale from 1-5 with 1 being Excellent health and 5 being poor health. The graph below illustrates the proportion of respondents rating their overall health stratified by their depression outcome (PHQ-2) status. We can observe that respondents who scored as Mild/No depression recorded their perceived overall health within the Excellent to Good range while respondents with Moderate/Severe depression were more likely to report their overall health within the Good to Poor range.

Social Network Size

Visualizing any differences in depression outcome by predictor, social network size - stratified by gender.

  • The graph below shows the mean social network size for different age groups stratified by gender and depression risk. From the graphs we can observe that female respondents at a low risk of depression are much likely to have a greater social network size on average as compared to females with a higher risk for depression. We also observe that females, in general, have a greater social network group as compared to males, controlling for depression risk.

Self rated memory

Visualizing any differences in depression outcome by predictor, self-rated memory.

  • Survey respondents were asked to rate their memory on a scale of 1-5, 1 being Excellent and 5 being Poor. This plot is a visualization of the proportion of respondents in this dataset by their depression outcome status - Low Depression Risk or Elevated Depression Risk. The figure is broken down by how participants rated their memory (x-axis).

Use of cane walker or wheelchair

Visualizing any differences in depression outcome by predictor, use of assistive devices (cane, walker, wheelchair).

  • Survey respondents were asked if they used a cane walker wheelchair. The plot below is a visualization of the proportion of respondents in this dataset by their depression outcome status - Mild/No Depression or Moderate/Severe Depression. The figure is stratified by if participants responded Yes, or No (x-axis) using a cane, walker, or wheelchare. There doesn’t appear to be a difference in proportion of respondents use or don’t use an assistive device among those who had elevated depression risk. Among those who had a low risk of depression scores, we see that there is a higher proportion respondents that do use an assistive device.
